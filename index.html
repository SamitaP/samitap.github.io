to analyze role of pollution in climate changes.

Overview.

This webpage helps users to compare temperature changes occurred over the years in most polluted countries and least polluted countries in the world, to identify if there is any relation between pollution and global warming. The most polluted and least polluted countries’ list is obtained from an article published by ‘The Telegraph’ on January 2nd, 2017. This system is mainly divided in three sections, first section focuses on visualizing temperature changes in most polluted countries, second section focuses on least polluted countries and third section focuses on forecasting and visualizing predicted temperature in future based on the available historic data.

Conclusion.

In all the graphs we can see fluctuations in earth’s surface temperatures over the years, however there is a significant growth in temperature some of the most polluted countries as compared to the least polluted countries over the years. Also on the other hand even in least polluted countries like Australia, New Zealand and Greenland etc. temperature has increased and is still increasing. Therefore, based on the historic data and visualizations we can conclude that pollution created by some countries are not just affecting temperature change in only those countries, but is impacting warming of the planet globally.
